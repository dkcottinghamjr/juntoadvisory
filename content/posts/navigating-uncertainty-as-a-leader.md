## The Myth of Perfect Information

Every leader faces the same tension: the need to act decisively and the reality that you never have complete information. The organizations that win aren't the ones that wait for certainty — they're the ones that build decision-making frameworks robust enough to handle ambiguity.

## Three Principles for Leading Through Fog

**1. Separate reversible from irreversible decisions.** Not every choice deserves the same level of deliberation. Reversible decisions should be made quickly. Irreversible ones deserve careful analysis — but even then, waiting too long is itself a decision.

**2. Build feedback loops, not prediction models.** Instead of trying to forecast the future, create systems that detect change quickly. The faster you can sense and respond, the less you need to predict.

**3. Embrace optionality.** The best strategies preserve the ability to adapt. When you're uncertain, invest in options — small bets that give you the right (but not the obligation) to scale.
