## Why Clarity Compounds

In most organizations, ambiguity is the default. Strategy documents are vague. Roles overlap. Priorities shift without explanation. The cost of this isn't dramatic — it's incremental. A little wasted effort here, a misaligned team there. Over months and years, these micro-costs compound into massive inefficiency.

Clarity is the antidote. When everyone understands the mission, their role in it, and the metrics that matter, something remarkable happens: people make better autonomous decisions. They move faster. They waste less energy on internal coordination and more on creating value.

## Where to Start

**Strategy:** Can every team member articulate the company's top three priorities? If not, the strategy isn't clear — it's aspirational.

**Roles:** Are ownership boundaries explicit? Overlap breeds politics. Gaps breed inaction.

**Metrics:** Does each team know what "winning" looks like in concrete, measurable terms?
